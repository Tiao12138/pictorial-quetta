QUETTA, 2022

In cooperation with: Bram Willems, Tessa van Abkoude, and Marc Wijkmans

An exhibition that stimulates conversation and makes the internet and its impact on the environment less abstract and more understandable. With the aim stimulating (the design of) thoughtful internet consumption. It consists of three exhibits. These are: (a) Infographic; poster about the internet infrastructure and interesting facts.(b) WiFinder; a device that can detect Wi-Fi strength and translates it into sound and vibration, (c) Shop your data; a product where you can input your data usage for a day and receive a receipt about the environmental impact it has.

"EVOLUTION"

The issue of climate change is considered by many to be one of the most significant crises of our era. Although it is widely acknowledged that industries such as aviation, transportation, and agriculture make significant contributions to this problem, the effect of the internet on the environment is frequently disregarded.

STATISTICS & CALCULATE

The internet is expanding at a rapid pace, resulting in a significant increase in the volume of online data traffic. However, the vast majority of the energy required to facilitate this traffic is generated from non-renewable sources, thereby putting our environment at risk.

How can we design for moderating internet usage in daily life?

DESIGN SPACE

To begin, it's crucial to raise awareness of the issue through a fun and engaging public display that offers personalized information on the environmental impact of internet usage.

Design Concept

The concept of sustainability related to the internet can be difficult to grasp due to its intangible nature. This presents an opportunity for designers to create products and experiences that render this issue more tangible. By utilizing a discursive design approach, an exhibition called Quetta has been developed. The exhibition aims to encourage thoughtful consumption of the internet by sparking conversations and making the environmental impact of the internet more comprehensible. Quetta consists of three exhibits:

(a) “Posters”, an Infographic poster detailing interesting facts about the internet's infrastructure.
(b) “WiFinder”, a device that detects Wi-Fi strength and translates it into sound and vibration, and
(c) “Shop your data”, a product that allows users to input their daily data usage and receive a receipt detailing its environmental impact.

"Posters"

poster in our exhibit educates visitors on the basics of the internet's infrastructure, including a world map showing global data center distribution and a storyboard demonstrating how the internet works when streaming a YouTube video in the Netherlands. On the other side, there are interesting facts and tips on reducing energy consumption. It provides an idea of how polluting the internet system can be.
